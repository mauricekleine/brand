# Maurice's Brand Voice Guide

## Core Voice Statement

Tone: casual, direct, internet-native. Prioritize clarity over polish, progress over perfection, and proof over hype. Write like a builder talking to other builders.

This is the anchor. Every post, landing page, changelog, tweet, email or announcement should feel like it came from someone who is actively building and shipping.

---

## Voice Pillars

### 1. Builder-First Mentality

Always speak from inside the process.

**Do**
- Share what you shipped
- Mention constraints, tradeoffs, iterations
- Show work in progress

**Avoid**
- Marketing fluff
- Corporate phrasing
- Vague announcements

### 2. Proof Over Hype

Let results and concrete actions speak.

**Do**
- Use numbers, facts, screenshots
- Mention real outcomes

**Avoid**
- Overpromising
- Buzzwords
- Future-heavy language

‚úÖ Good: just crossed $750 MRR
‚ùå Bad: positioned for exponential growth

---

### 3. Casual, Internet-Native Tone

Write like you belong on X, Discord and GitHub.

**Style traits**
- lowercase acceptable
- short lines
- natural spacing
- occasional memes
- light emojis when appropriate

‚úÖ Good: shortest feature freeze ever
‚ùå Bad: This was an unexpectedly short product stabilization period

---

### 4. Understated Confidence

Never brag. Never apologize for success either.

**Do**
- State facts calmly
- Let the absurdity speak for itself

**Avoid**
- Ego language
- Performative humility

‚ùå Bad: I can't believe I managed to build something this amazing

---

### 5. Curious Tinkerer Energy

Show experimentation and exploration.

**Do**
- Share experiments
- Admit when things break
- Highlight learning moments

‚úÖ Good: this broke in production, fixed it, learned something new
‚ùå Bad: All systems performed optimally as expected

## Vocabulary Guidelines

### Preferred Words
ship, build, fix, break, test, launch, tweak, experiment, hack, iterate

### Avoid Words
leverage, ecosystem, synergy, robust, solution, enterprise-grade, scalable, platform, next-generation

## Platform Adjustments

### X / Twitter
- Casual
- Playful
- Fast updates
- Memes acceptable

Goal: visibility + builder credibility

### LinkedIn
- Slightly more polished
- Still personal
- Still builder-first

Goal: professional credibility without corporate tone

### Product Changelogs / Announcements
- Direct
- Transparent
- Honest

‚úÖ Good: this was buggy yesterday. it's not anymore.

### Landing Pages
- Simple
- Outcome-focused
- No fluff

‚úÖ Good: Create realistic chat mockups in seconds.
‚ùå Bad: Empowering creators through next-gen visualization tools

## Example Voice Transformations

### Marketing Copy

**Before:** We are proud to introduce a powerful new feature set.
**After:** shipped 3 new features today. they solve real problems people asked for.

### Feature Announcement

**Before:** Our engineering team has deployed improvements.
**After:** fixed the thing that annoyed everyone yesterday.

## North Star Test

Before publishing, ask:

- Does this sound like a builder talking to builders?
- Is this concrete?
- Would I say this out loud on Discord?
- Does it feel honest?
